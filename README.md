## **Project Proposal: Celebrate - An event management and marketplace platform**

## **1. Problem Statement**

Organizing events such as naming ceremonies, birthdays, anniversaries, weddings, and receptions involves multiple complex tasks including planning, design, logistics, vendor coordination, budgeting, risk handling, execution, and post-event wrap-up.

Currently, individuals and families rely heavily on referrals from friends, relatives, or social media to choose an event management team. This approach presents several limitations:

- Limited Exploration: Clients do not have sufficient time or access to evaluate multiple event management teams.
- Lack of Comparison: There is no structured way to compare different teams based on services, pricing, and design quality.
- Design Uncertainty: Clients cannot visualize how their event—especially key elements like the dais (stage decoration)—will look before committing.
- Budget vs Expectation Mismatch: Without clear comparisons and previews, clients may end up compromising either on budget or satisfaction.

As a result, decision-making is inefficient, subjective, and often leads to suboptimal outcomes.

## **2. Proposed Solution**

This project introduces a centralized platform that enables clients to describe their event requirements and receive tailored proposals from multiple event management teams.

### Core Functionality

- Clients provide:
    - Event type and details
    - Needs and expectations
    - Budget constraints
    - Location (with or without a predefined venue)
- Smart Planner Matching Engine:
    - **Location-Based Filtering:** Filters planners based on the event location to identify serviceable candidates.
    - **Semantic Requirement Analysis:** Uses Natural Language Processing (NLP) to understand client requirements expressed in English, Tamil, or mixed-language inputs.
    - **Planner Similarity Matching:** Compares client requirements with planner profiles, expertise, and portfolio information to identify the best matches.
    - **Automated Request Distribution:** Distributes event requests to matched planners and generates notifications for proposal submission.
- Event management teams/multiple vendors respond with:
    - Detailed execution plans
    - Budget breakdowns
    - Most importantly, visual designs of the dais/event setup
- **Collaborative Event Design Workspace**
    - **Visual Proposal Submission** – Planners submit event designs alongside proposals.
    - **Client Co-Creation Tools** – Clients can customize planner designs through an interactive design workspace.
    - **Version-Based Collaboration** – All design revisions are tracked and managed through version history.
    - **Design-Driven Booking Decisions** – Clients finalize planners based on collaboratively refined event concepts rather than static proposals alone.

This creates a competitive and transparent environment where multiple teams present their best concepts aligned with the client’s requirements.

## **3. Key Scenarios (Edge Cases)**

### Scenario 1: Client Has a Predefined Venue

- The client specifies the event hall or dais.
- Multiple event management teams design and propose solutions based on the given venue.

### Scenario 2: Client Has No Venue Selected

- The client provides budget, requirements, and location only.
- Event management teams:
    - Select suitable venues within the location
    - Provide complete end-to-end plans including venue, design, and execution

## 4. Unique Value Proposition

- **AI-Powered Planner Matching:** Clients describe their event requirements in natural language, and Celebrate intelligently identifies planners whose expertise, portfolio, and service offerings best match the request.
- **Automatic Requirement Distribution:** Event requests are automatically delivered to relevant planners, eliminating the need for clients to manually search, contact, and compare dozens of vendors.
- **Collaborative Event Design Workspace:** Planners submit visual event concepts and décor designs along with their proposals. Clients can further customize these designs using the platform's design tool and planner-provided assets, enabling collaborative event creation before booking.
- **Design Version Tracking:** Every design modification is stored as a version, creating a transparent design history between the client and planner and reducing misunderstandings during execution.
- **Visual-First Decision Making:** Clients evaluate actual event concepts, décor layouts, and stage designs rather than relying solely on descriptions, portfolios, or promises.
- **Multi-Team Competitive Proposals:** Multiple planners compete for the same event, encouraging higher-quality concepts, better pricing, and greater transparency.
- **Personalized Event Planning:** Every proposal is tailored to the client's budget, location, theme preferences, guest count, and specific requirements rather than relying on predefined packages.
- **End-to-End Flexibility:** Supports both scenarios where clients already have a venue and where planners must recommend venues and provide complete event solutions.

## 5. Solution Summary

Celebrate is an AI-enabled event planning marketplace that combines intelligent planner matching, collaborative design creation, and proposal-based competition within a single platform.

Clients submit their event requirements, including budget, location, event type, and expectations. The Smart Planner Matching Engine analyzes these requirements and automatically identifies planners whose expertise, portfolios, and service areas align with the client's needs. Event requests are then distributed directly to these planners.

Matched planners prepare customized proposals containing execution plans, budget estimates, venue recommendations (if required), and visual décor concepts for the event. Rather than simply reviewing static designs, clients can interact with these concepts through the platform's design workspace, modify elements using planner-provided assets, and share revised versions back with planners.

The platform maintains a version history of all design iterations, enabling transparent collaboration between clients and planners before any booking decision is made. This ensures that expectations, creative preferences, and design requirements are aligned prior to finalizing an agreement.

By combining intelligent planner discovery, automated requirement distribution, collaborative design editing, design version management, visual proposal comparison, and competitive bidding, Celebrate transforms event planning from a referral-based and fragmented process into a transparent, interactive, and data-driven experience for both clients and event professionals.

## 8. Business Model

### Beta Phase (Validation Stage)

During the initial phase, Celebrate will operate as a non-profit validation platform focused on testing product-market fit, user adoption, and workflow effectiveness.

The platform will be deployed within college campuses to support the organization of technical, cultural, and club events.

In this ecosystem:

- Department committees, student organizers, and club coordinators act as clients.
- College event management teams, design teams, and organizing committees act as planners.
- Users can create event requirements, receive proposals, collaborate on event designs, and manage event planning workflows without any platform charges.

The primary objective of this phase is to:

- Validate the Smart Planner Matching Engine.
- Evaluate the Collaborative Event Design Workspace.
- Measure user engagement and proposal conversion rates.
- Gather feedback for iterative product improvements.
- Assess the long-term commercial viability of the platform.

**Reasoning:**

- Eliminates adoption barriers during validation.
- Creates both demand and supply within a controlled environment.
- Enables rapid product iteration using real event scenarios.
- Generates operational insights before entering the commercial market.

### Stable Phase (Post Product-Market Fit)

After successful validation and product refinement, Celebrate will expand into the professional event management market, connecting individual clients with event planners, decorators, and event management companies.

The platform will adopt a subscription-based model for planners.

### Basic Plan

- Access to the Smart Planner Matching Engine.
- Receive event leads and proposal opportunities generated through the platform.
- Submit proposals and communicate through the platform.
- No access to the AI Design Canvas.

### Premium Plan

Includes all Basic Plan features, plus:

- Access to the AI-Powered Event Design Canvas.
- Ability to create, edit, and share visual event concepts.
- Access to collaborative client-planner design workflows.
- Design version management and asset libraries.
- Enhanced proposal presentation capabilities.

Clients can submit event requirements, compare proposals, and participate in collaborative design discussions without any subscription fee.

**Reasoning:**

- Creates a low-cost entry point for planners through the Basic Plan.
- Monetizes advanced design and visualization capabilities through the Premium Plan.
- Encourages planner adoption while generating recurring revenue.
- Aligns pricing directly with the value delivered to planners.
- Supports scalable growth without introducing friction for clients.

This model establishes Celebrate as a SaaS-enabled event marketplace where planner subscriptions fund platform growth while clients benefit from transparent proposal comparison, intelligent planner matching, and collaborative event design.

## **10. Competitive Advantage**

Your strongest differentiator is the combination. Existing platforms separate the workflow into marketplace discovery, venue booking, planning checklists, or visualization tools, while your proposal imagines one workflow where a client posts requirements and multiple event teams return customized plans, budgets, and visual concepts for the same brief.

That combined model appears less common in mainstream products, especially for Indian family events beyond weddings, such as naming ceremonies, birthdays, anniversaries, and receptions. Sharetribe’s marketplace guidance even describes proposal sharing, quote management, mood boards, and multiple quote versions as core needs for event marketplaces, which indirectly supports your architecture.

## 11. Long-Term Vision

The long-term vision of Celebrate is to evolve from a digital event proposal and planning platform into a technology-enabled event management ecosystem. The journey begins with gaining practical experience by actively participating in and understanding the complete lifecycle of event execution, including planning, logistics, coordination, budgeting, risk management, and on-site operations.

As an initial validation step, Celebrate will be piloted within college campuses to streamline the organization of hackathons, technical events, cultural programs, and club activities. This will provide real-world feedback on event coordination challenges while allowing the platform to mature through iterative improvements.

Over time, the platform aims to become the operating system for event management, enabling organizers to plan, visualize, coordinate, and execute events through a single solution. Alongside the software platform, Celebrate will expand into a professional event management organization that leverages its technology, operational expertise, and vendor network to deliver end-to-end event experiences. The ultimate goal is to build a trusted brand that combines event management services with innovative technology, creating a scalable ecosystem for both organizers and event professionals.

## **SWOT analysis**

## **Strengths**

- **Strong pain point: today’s process is fragmented across referrals, WhatsApp, Instagram, venue visits, and manual quote comparison. Existing platforms validate that users want centralized discovery, planning, and vendor comparison.**
- **Clear visual value: tools like Weddie, VenuePreview, BrideVue, Prismm, and Social Tables show that visualizing décor and layout before commitment is commercially meaningful.**
- **Good fit for India: platforms such as WeddingBazaar, WeddingWire India, and Weddingz.in prove strong digital adoption for wedding and event sourcing in the Indian market.**

## **Weaknesses**

- **Hard operational model: getting event teams to create custom proposals and visuals for every lead is more labor-intensive than running a simple directory or quote form.**
- **Quality control risk: proposal comparison only works if plans, pricing, assumptions, and deliverables are normalized across vendors; otherwise users compare non-equivalent offers. Sharetribe’s guidance on quote versioning and detailed proposals suggests this complexity is real.**
- **Visual trust gap: AI or mockup-based décor previews can raise expectation mismatches if the final execution differs from the concept. Products in this space promise visualization, but execution still depends on vendors.**

## **Opportunities**

- **Integration gap: there is room for a platform that merges vendor competition, venue recommendation, planning workflows, and visual preview into one consumer product. Existing tools tend to specialize.**
- **Tier-2 and vernacular growth: India’s event market is broad, and most mainstream wedding-tech brands remain wedding-heavy rather than family-event-heavy. A multilingual or regional-first model could stand out. Marketplaces already show scale across Indian cities, suggesting room for category expansion.**
- **SaaS + marketplace hybrid: you could earn from lead fees, subscription tools for planners, premium visualization, and transaction commissions, similar to how planning marketplaces and vendor platforms create value.**

## **Threats**

- **Incumbent platforms can copy features: WeddingBazaar, Weddingz.in, WeddingWire, and The Knot already own user traffic, vendor networks, and wedding search intent.**
- **Specialist visualization tools may absorb this layer faster: Prismm, BrideVue, Weddie, and VenuePreview already focus on design preview and spatial/decor visualization.**
- **Marketplace chicken-and-egg problem: clients need many quality vendors, and vendors only join when high-intent leads already exist. This is a classic risk for service marketplaces.**

## **Assessment**

The project idea does exist in parts, and the broad category is already in active use through wedding marketplaces, planning apps, and event visualization software.

---

A practical positioning line for your proposal would be: this project is not the first wedding/event platform, but it addresses a gap left by current systems that are mostly directories, planning tools, or visualization apps rather than a unified proposal competition platform.

What appears less saturated is the exact product position: a proposal-comparison platform for events where multiple planners compete on plan, budget, and venue-specific design preview in one standardized flow. That gives your project a credible innovation angle, but it should be framed as “an integrated event proposal marketplace with visual pre-commitment”, not as a completely unprecedented idea.
